A Parent & Student Planning Guide

The College Apartment Checklist

Everything your student actually needs to move in, settle in, and thrive — organized room by room, with the true essentials flagged so you buy smart, not twice.

Sending a child to college is a milestone — and a logistics project. We built this checklist for the families we work with so the move-in is calm instead of chaotic. Start with The First 20% below (the items that make the apartment livable on day one), then work through the room-by-room list. Tip: measure the space and confirm what's already provided before you shop.

Before You Buy — Pro Tips

A few minutes of planning prevents the most common (and expensive) move-in mistakes.
Measure first.Bed size + mattress depth, closet layout, and room dimensions before ordering furniture or bins.
Confirm what's provided.Microwave, dishwasher, washer/dryer, ice maker — don't double-buy what the unit already has.
Divide, don't split.With roommates, each person owns shared items outright — no cost-splitting to untangle at move-out.
Choose safe materials.Look for OEKO-TEX® textiles and CertiPUR-US certified bedding for non-toxic peace of mind.
Check the rules.Wall-hanging and mounting policies vary — confirm before buying art, shelves, or command hooks.
Storage is the secret.Vertical and under-bed storage turns a small apartment into a calm, functional home.
